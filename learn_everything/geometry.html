Geometry

Lines and Angles

Lines can be parallel, intersecting, or skew. Angles can be acute, right, obtuse, or straight.

Examples:

  • Parallel lines never intersect.
  • A right angle is \( 90^\circ \).

Triangles

Triangles can be categorized based on sides and angles.

Examples:

  • Equilateral triangle: All sides are equal.
  • Isosceles triangle: At least two sides are equal.
  • Scalene triangle: No sides are equal.

Circles

A circle is a shape with all points the same distance from its center.

Examples:

  • Radius: The distance from the center to a point on the circle.
  • Diameter: The distance across the circle through the center.

Quadrilaterals

Quadrilaterals are four-sided figures.

Examples:

  • Square: All sides are equal, and all angles are \(90^\circ\).
  • Rectangle: Opposite sides are equal, and all angles are \(90^\circ\).
  • Parallelogram: Opposite sides are equal, and opposite angles are equal.
