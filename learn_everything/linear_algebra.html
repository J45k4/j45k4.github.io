Linear Algebra

Matrices

Matrices are grids of numbers that can be added, subtracted, and multiplied.

Examples:

  • Identity matrix: \( I \)
  • Inverse matrix: \( A^{-1} \)

Vectors

Vectors have direction and magnitude and are often represented as arrows.

Examples:

  • Column vector: \( \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix} \)
  • Row vector: \( \begin{pmatrix} x, y \end{pmatrix} \)

Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors

These are special vectors and values associated with a matrix.

Examples:

  • Eigenvalue equation: \( A\vec{x} = \lambda\vec{x} \)
  • Eigenvector for a 2x2 matrix.
