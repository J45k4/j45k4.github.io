Abstract Algebra

Groups

Groups are sets equipped with an operation that combines any two elements to form a third element.

Examples:

  • Cyclic groups
  • Symmetry groups

Rings

Rings are sets that are equipped with two operations: addition and multiplication.

Examples:

  • Integers
  • Polynomial rings

Fields

A field is a set with two operations: addition and multiplication, which behave much like those for rational and real numbers.

Examples:

  • Real numbers
  • Complex numbers
