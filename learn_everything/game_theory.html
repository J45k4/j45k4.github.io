Game Theory

Zero-Sum Games

These are games where one person's gain is another's loss.

Examples:

  • Chess
  • Poker

Non-Zero-Sum Games

These are games where the outcome can benefit both or all players.

Examples:

  • Prisoner's Dilemma
  • Tragedy of the Commons

Nash Equilibrium

This is a situation where no player can benefit by changing their strategy while the other players keep theirs unchanged.

Examples:

  • Bidding strategies
  • Market competition
