Algebra

Basic Algebraic Expressions

Algebraic expressions are math phrases that can have numbers, operators, and variables.

Examples:

  • \( 5x + 3 \)
  • \( 2y - 7 \)
  • \( 3a + 4b - c \)

Equations

An equation is a statement that two expressions are equal.

Examples:

  • \( 2x + 3 = 7 \)
  • \( y - 5 = 10 \)
  • \( 3a + 4b = 12 \)

Inequalities

Inequalities describe an expression that may be less than or greater than another.

Examples:

  • \( x > 5 \)
  • \( y \leq 8 \)
  • \( a + b \geq 10 \)

Polynomials

Polynomials are algebraic expressions that consist of terms in the form \( ax^n \).

Examples:

  • \( 3x^2 + 2x - 4 \)
  • \( 4y^3 - y^2 + 7 \)
  • \( 5a^2 - 3a + 1 \)
