Pre-Calculus

Functions

Functions map input values to output values.

Examples:

  • Linear Functions: \( y = mx + b \)
  • Quadratic Functions: \( y = ax^2 + bx + c \)

Limits

Limits help us understand the behavior of functions as they approach a certain point.

Examples:

  • \( \lim_{{x \to 0}} \frac{{\sin(x)}}{x} = 1 \)

Complex Numbers

Complex numbers have both a real and an imaginary part.

Examples:

  • \( a + bi \) where \( a \) is real and \( b \) is imaginary.
